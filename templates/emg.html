Emergency: Must know

The World Health Organization (WHO) recognizes several emergency conditions and situations that can occur during pregnancy and after giving birth

During Pregnancy:

Severe Preeclampsia and Eclampsia: Preeclampsia is a condition characterized by high blood pressure and organ damage during pregnancy.

    

Gestational Diabetes Complications: Complications arising from gestational diabetes, such as uncontrolled high blood sugar levels, can pose risks to both.

    

Preterm Labor: Preeclampsia is a condition characterized by high blood pressure and organ damage during pregnancy.

    

Antepartum Hemorrhage: This refers to significant bleeding during pregnancy, which can be caused by conditions like placenta previa or placental abruption

    

During Labor and Childbirth:

Obstructed Labor: This occurs when the baby's head is too large or positioned in a way that prevents it from passing through the birth canal.

    

Postpartum Hemorrhage: Excessive bleeding after childbirth is a critical emergency situation that can lead to severe complications or death if not promptly managed.

    

After Childbirth (Postpartum Period):

Puerperal Sepsis:Infection that occurs after childbirth due to unhygienic conditions or inadequate medical care can lead to sepsis, a life-threatening condition.

    

Postpartum Eclampsia:Eclampsia can also occur in the postpartum period and requires immediate medical attention.

    

Peripartum Cardiomyopathy:A condition where the heart weakens during pregnancy or shortly after childbirth, which can lead to heart failure.

    

Thromboembolic Disorders: These include conditions like deep vein thrombosis (DVT) and pulmonary embolism (PE), which can occur due to increased risk factors during pregnancy and the postpartum period.
